📊 E-Commerce Analytics — Business Findings Report

Dataset:Olist Brazilian E-Commerce (2016–2018)  
Analyst: Golam Rabbi  
Tools: Python (pandas, matplotlib, seaborn) · PostgreSQL · Power BI

Executive Summary: 


Analysis of 96,000+ delivered orders across a 20-month period reveals four 
critical business problems: a customer retention crisis, geographic logistics 
failures, freight margin risk in key categories, and a direct causal link 
between delivery delays and review score collapse.


Finding 1 — Customer Retention Crisis (Most Critical)

The cohort retention analysis reveals near-zero repeat purchase behavior 
across all customer cohorts. Retention drops to effectively 0–1% by Month 1 
across every cohort from 2017-02 through 2018-08.

Implication: The business is running entirely on new customer acquisition.
Every revenue gain is offset by the cost of replacing churned customers.
A 5% improvement in Month-1 retention would compound significantly 
given the current order volume scale.

Recommendation: Introduce a post-purchase loyalty trigger — targeted  email or app push within 7 days of delivery offering a category-specific discount based on first purchase behavior.



Finding 2 — Delivery Delay Directly Destroys Review Scores:

On-time deliveries average 4.08 out of 5 . Orders delayed by just 5–10 days  average 1.83 out of 5 which is a 55% score collapse.  Orders delayed 20+ days scored 1.75 .

Finding 3 — Geographic Logistics Failure in Northeast Brazil:


States AL (Alagoas) and MA (Maranhão) have late delivery rates  exceeding 20% against a national average of 9.9%. Both are Northeast states with underdeveloped logistics infrastructure.

SP (São Paulo) generates ~R$7M revenue 40%+ of total platform revenue while also maintaining below-average late delivery rates, confirming infrastructure quality correlates with business performance.

Recommendation: Audit last-mile carrier contracts for AL, MA, SE, CE. Consider regional fulfillment partnerships or longer estimated delivery windows for Northeast customers to manage expectations.





Finding 4 — Freight Margin Risk in Select Categories: 


21.6% of all order items have freight costs exceeding 30% of total 
order value. Two significant categories cross this threshold at scale.

Finding 5 — Payment Behavior & Revenue Concentration:


Credit card drives 73.9% of orders and R$14M+ in revenue. Credit card buyers have the highest AOV at ~R$178 vs voucher buyers at ~R$130. Boleto (bank slip) represents a significant unbanked customer segment at 19% of orders worth R$4M.

Recommendation: Prioritize credit card UX optimization and installment plan promotion these buyers spend more and likely have higher LTV potential.

Finding 6 — Peak Ordering Window (Marketing Timing)


Order volume peaks Monday–Wednesday between 10am–4pm (Brasília time). Weekend volume is 30–40% lower than weekday peaks. Early morning  (midnight–8am) is near-zero across all days.

Recommendation: Concentrate paid marketing spend and promotional pushes in the Mon–Wed 10am–3pm window for maximum conversion efficiency.

Technical Notes: 


- Dataset: 99,441 orders, 9 relational tables, 100k+ order items
- Analysis period: 2017-02 to 2018-08 (launch noise removed)
- 667 duplicate review records identified and deduplicated
- 13 untranslated product categories manually mapped
- All analysis conducted on delivered orders only (96,478 records)
